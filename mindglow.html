🎗️ What is Cancer?

Our bodies are made of billions of tiny things called cells. Normally, cells grow, do their job, and then stop. But sometimes, some cells go a little wrong and start growing way too fast — like they forgot to stop! That's what we call cancer.

🔬 There are more than 100 different types of cancer. Doctors have special names for each one!

Cancer can happen in almost any part of the body — like the skin, blood, lungs, or brain. Doctors use special tests like X-rays and blood tests to find it.

💡 Did you know? Many people get better from cancer when it's found early and treated by doctors!

Treatments can include medicines, special beams of light, or surgery. It can be tough, but many kids and adults beat cancer and go on to live full, happy lives! 💪

⚕️ If you notice something unusual about your body, always tell a grown-up. Catching things early makes a big difference!

🧠 What is a Brain Tumor?

Your brain is the most amazing control center in the whole world — it controls everything you do, think, and feel! A brain tumor is when extra cells grow inside the brain and start taking up space where they shouldn't be.

🔍 Doctors can find brain tumors using special cameras called MRI and CT scans — they take pictures of the inside of your head!

Not all brain tumors are the same. Some grow slowly and might not need treatment right away. Others need surgery or special medicines. Doctors decide the best plan for each person.

💡 Signs to tell a grown-up about: very bad headaches that don't go away, blurry vision, or feeling dizzy a lot.

The most important thing is to tell a trusted adult if something doesn't feel right. Doctors are really good at helping! 💛

🤕 Why Do We Get Headaches?

Headaches are super common — almost everyone gets one sometimes! A headache is when your head feels sore, tight, or painful. There are lots of reasons this happens.

💧 The #1 most common reason for headaches in kids is not drinking enough water!

Other reasons include: being too tired, skipping meals, staring at a screen too long, being stressed, or even being in a loud, bright place. Most headaches go away with a little rest and water. 🌊

💡 The 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds to rest your eyes!

If you get really bad headaches very often, or they feel different from usual, make sure to tell a grown-up so a doctor can check. 💛

🌙 Why Is Sleep a Superpower?

When you're asleep, it might feel like nothing is happening — but actually, your brain and body are working super hard! Sleep is when your brain stores memories from the day, your muscles heal and grow, and your body gets ready for tomorrow.

😴 Kids aged 6–12 need 9 to 11 hours of sleep every single night!

Not sleeping enough can make you feel grumpy, make it harder to learn, give you headaches, and even make you feel more worried or sad. Sleep is like charging your body battery! 🔋

💡 Try going to bed at the same time every night — your body loves a routine!

Tips for better sleep: put screens away 30 minutes before bed, keep your room cool and dark, and try reading a book or taking deep breaths to calm down. 💛

💧 Why Does Water Matter So Much?

Did you know your body is about 60% water? That means water is literally what you're mostly made of! Every single part of your body — your brain, blood, muscles, and skin — needs water to work properly.

🧠 Even being a little bit dehydrated can make it harder to think and concentrate in school!

When you don't drink enough water, you might get headaches, feel tired, feel dizzy, or have trouble focusing. It's one of the easiest health problems to fix — just drink more water!

💡 A good goal: drink 6 to 8 glasses of water every day. More if you've been running around!

Tip: Carry a water bottle with you everywhere. If your pee is dark yellow, it means you need more water! 💧

😟 What is Stress and Worry?

Stress is what you feel when something seems hard, scary, or too much to handle. Everyone gets stressed sometimes — even grown-ups! It's a normal feeling, but it's important to know how to deal with it.

🫀 When you feel stressed, your heart beats faster and your body gets ready to react — it's called the "fight or flight" response!

Signs of too much stress: headaches, tummy aches, trouble sleeping, feeling sad or angry, or not wanting to do things you normally like. Sound familiar? That's okay — you can feel better!

💡 Taking 5 slow, deep breaths actually calms down your brain and helps you feel less stressed — it works every time!

The most important thing is to talk to someone you trust — a parent, teacher, or school counselor. You don't have to carry big feelings alone. 💛
